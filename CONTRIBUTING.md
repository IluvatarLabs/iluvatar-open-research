# Contributing to IORI

Every contribution is welcome. You don't need to be a domain expert to participate.

## Four ways to contribute

### 1. Propose a project

[Submit a question](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/issues/new?template=new-project-idea.yml). Propose a research question you think Marvin should run. Include:
- The research question (one sentence)
- Why it matters (2-3 sentences)
- What public data exists (bulleted list with accession numbers or URLs)
- What validation would look like

We reply to every submission with specific feedback, whether or not it's selected.

### 2. Review an active project

[Open a critique](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/issues/new?template=scientific-critique.yml). Push back on an interpretation, flag a confounder we missed, or challenge a confidence classification. We take every critique seriously: if it holds, we revise the finding; if it doesn't, we document why in the project's permanent record.

### 3. Share a resource

Two templates depending on what you're sharing:

- **Dataset**: [Suggest a dataset](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/issues/new?template=new-data-source.yml). Point us at a public dataset we missed, share curated or processed data, or contribute validation results. Include accession numbers or URLs.
- **Paper or direction**: [Suggest a paper](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/issues/new?template=literature-suggestion.yml). Flag a paper we should have cited, propose a hypothesis Marvin should test, or suggest a method worth trying.

### 4. Run a validation

[Offer to validate](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/issues/new?template=validation-offer.yml). Tell us which hypothesis you want to test, what experimental or analytical system you'd use, and your estimated timeline. We'll coordinate to avoid duplicate effort and ensure you get full CRediT attribution on resulting publications.

This is the highest-value contribution IORI can receive. It's what turns a computational prediction into confirmed or refuted science.

## Not sure where to start?

Browse the [active projects](https://iluvatarlabs.github.io/iori/#projects) on the IORI website, read the findings and open questions, and see what resonates. Or start a [Discussion](https://github.com/IluvatarLabs/iluvatar-open-research/discussions) if you want to brainstorm before filing a formal issue.

## How Marvin updates work

Marvin's iterations are delivered as pull requests against the relevant project directory. Each PR includes a structured summary: what changed, what datasets were added, which hypotheses were revised or retracted, and what open questions remain.

PRs sit open for **7 days** before merge so the community can review the diff, comment on specific changes, and flag concerns. After the review period, the PR merges and the project manifest is updated.

## Attribution

We use the [CRediT](https://credit.niso.org/) (Contributor Roles Taxonomy) framework:

- **Submitting researcher**: Conceptualization, Supervision, plus any roles actively filled
- **Iluvatar / Marvin team**: Methodology, Software, Formal Analysis, Data Curation, Visualization
- **Community contributors**: credited for specific roles based on actual contributions
- **Corresponding author**: submitting researcher (community projects) or Iluvatar lead scientist (showcase projects)
- **Marvin**: acknowledged in the methods section, not listed as an author

## Code of conduct

Be rigorous, be specific, be respectful. Critique methods and findings, not people. If you disagree with a conclusion, show the evidence.
